How we made our 3rd prototype:

The first step we did was reverse engineer a finger pulse monitor.
The second step was to get all the pieces together and sketch them.

The third step was to assemble the pieces together and make a cardboard prototype.

The fourth step was to add some moving components to the prototype.
The fifth step was we bought the pieces we needed and started assembling the project.
Sadly, we didnt have all of the parts and it would take too long ot order the exact ones we needed.
Instead, we found alternative components online that all came last minute with Amazon Prime.

The Sixth step was getting the working prototype.
The seventh step was coding the heart rate monitor to make sure the display screen turned on.
The eighth step was making sure the pulse sensor measured the correct pulse of a human when putting their finger on the scanner.
The ninth step was checking to make sure the data is correct and make sure it is able to locate a pulse.
The tenth step was putting our finger on the scanner and make sure it works correctly.
